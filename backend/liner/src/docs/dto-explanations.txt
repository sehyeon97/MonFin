The files named:
1. transaction-data.dto.ts (Payment Processor folder)
2. sales-history.dto.ts (Merchant folder)
3. order-history.dto.ts (Customer folder)

have the exact same fields. The reason they are the same but existing in different folders
is to show that merchant, customer, and payment processor is independent of each other.
The purpose of having it separated is to demonstrate Microservices Architecture concept,
without actually creating multiple projects for each service.

The same goes for the entities that revolve around these DTOs.
Customer, Merchant, and Payment Processor have their own tables, but not separate databases.
Therefore, fields will overlap and may seem redundant, and that is intentional.
Although not the exact way microservices are supposed to be implemented,
to minimize my personal cpu load and resources, everything is handled in one, with microservices logic.
